# Cover Specification

Shared across editions except for the **colourway**, which is edition-specific and forms a deliberate palette system.

## Shared (all editions)
- **Wordmark only** on the cover — no subtitle, tagline, or author line.
- **Finish:** silver foil stamp on the wordmark, optionally combined with a blind emboss (registered foil-emboss). No other foil, no decorative deboss.
- **Construction:** cloth (buckram) case preferred, or a heavy uncoated / duplexed board. **No matte film lamination** (it scuffs and fingerprints — wrong for an object about permanence).
- No gloss, no imagery.
- Keep the wordmark lockup centered, restrained, and small rather than large.
- Prove the finish with a **foil draw-down on the actual cover material** before the run.

## Colourway system (edition-specific)
| Edition | Cloth colour | Approx. reference |
|---|---|---|
| Man on Record | Black | ~#111111 |
| Woman on Record | Oxblood (deep blood-red) | ~#4A0E10 |
| The Common Record | Dark maroon (black bled with oxblood) | ~#2E1214 |

The logic: black (Man) and oxblood (Woman) are a complementary pair; The Common Record's dark maroon is the optical blend of the two — the shared book visibly belongs to both. Colours are austere and severe by intent; **not** soft or gendered.

Hex values are approximate — **confirm against physical bindery cloth swatches** (e.g., Arrestox / buckram ranges). Silver foil must be draw-down tested on each colour.
