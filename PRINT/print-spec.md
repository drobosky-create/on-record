# Print Specification — The Record (Seed Edition)

Send this exact spec to printers. No explanation attached.

## Binding
- Smyth-sewn / thread-sewn
- Lay-flat
- Non-refillable
- **No** spiral, disc, or glued-only binding

## Paper
- Interior: 100–120 GSM uncoated
- Off-white or bone
- No dot grid, no ruling unless supplied as a custom file

## Cover
- Matte black or charcoal
- **Title: embossed silver** (silver foil / metallic on the title only)
- No gloss
- No imagery
- No decorative embossing beyond the title treatment

## Quantity
- Initial run: **12 units**
- Small batch, no setup pressure

## Expected cost (reality check)
- ~$40–$80 per unit for a 12-unit run; possibly more depending on cover stock and proofing.
- A $10–$20/unit quote signals the wrong quality tier.

## Vendor screening
Reject any printer that:
- Does not know "Smyth-sewn" without Googling it
- Says "perfect bound" without clarifying it is sewn
- Hedges on whether the book lies flat without force
- Will not provide a physical proof before the full run
- Cannot name the uncoated off-white stock they carry
- Quotes turnaround longer than ~3–4 weeks for a 12-unit sewn run
- Pushes back on the small quantity

### Questions to ask verbatim
1. "Is the binding sewn or glued?"
2. "Will the book lie flat without force?"
3. "Can I approve a physical proof before the full run?"
4. "What paper brands do you carry in uncoated off-white?"
5. "What is the turnaround time for a 12-unit sewn run?"

### Control move
Request one physical sample of any sewn book the printer has produced (not ours). Feel it, open it, lay it flat. If the object does not communicate seriousness, move on.
