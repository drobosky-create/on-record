# Print Specification — The Record (Seed Edition)

Send this exact spec to binderies. No explanation attached.

## Edition
- **90-day edition.** Front matter (Identity Standard + Non-Negotiable Standards) → Initiation Days 1–7 → 90 Daily Pages → a Weekly Escalation Page after every 7th daily (~13).
- Finite, permanent, non-refillable.

## Opening behavior (hard requirement)
- **Genuine lay-flat** — pages stay open, flat to the gutter, hands-free, so the holder can write without fighting the book.

## Binding
- Preferred: **section-sewn with a lay-flat-capable spine** (hollow-back / drawn-on or exposed-spine construction that opens flat).
- Acceptable fallback if sewing cannot deliver true flat opening at this trim: **layflat (PUR) binding**.
- Sewing is preferred for permanence; lay-flat wins if the two conflict. Bindery to advise.
- **Not permitted:** spiral, disc, ring, or any refillable / removable-page binding.

## Paper
- Interior: 100–120 GSM uncoated, off-white / bone, with good ink holdout (write-tested — see below).
- No dot grid, no ruling unless supplied as a custom file.

## Cover
- **Cloth (buckram) case** preferred, or a heavy uncoated / duplexed board.
- **No matte film lamination** (scuffs and fingerprints — wrong for an object about permanence).
- Colour: black or charcoal. No gloss, no imagery.
- **Title: silver foil stamp**, optionally combined with a blind emboss (registered foil-emboss). No other foil, no decorative deboss.

## Trim
- Recommendation: ~140 × 210 mm (A5-ish) or 5 × 7.75 in — hand-held, upright book proportion, never a wide planner. Confirm against paper bulk and lay-flat behaviour.

## Quantity
- **2–3 hand-bound proofs**, then a **~50-unit seed run**.
- Note: a true 12-unit sewn run is uneconomical at edition binderies (minimums are far higher; hand-binding ~12 runs roughly $120–300/unit).

## Expected cost (reality check)
- Hand-bound proofs: premium, per-unit high.
- ~50-unit seed run: budget realistically for cloth case + foil + lay-flat binding; treat as a credibility investment, not a margin exercise.
- A $10–20/unit quote signals the wrong quality tier.

## Proofing & material tests (before any run)
1. **Blank bound dummy first** — exact stock and binding, no printing. Confirms lay-flat, feel, bulk, spine, cover finish.
2. **Write test** — the shipped pen on the chosen stock (feathering, show-through, dry time).
3. **Foil/emboss draw-down** on the actual cover material.
4. **Full physical proof** of the printed, bound book before the seed run.

## Vendor screening
The right partner is a short-run bookbinder / fine-press that serves publishers. Reject any who:
- Cannot deliver genuine lay-flat.
- Say "perfect bound" without clarifying construction.
- Will not provide a bound dummy and a physical proof.
- Cannot name the uncoated off-white stock they carry.
- Push you toward gloss, matte film lamination, or decorative extras.

### Questions to ask verbatim
1. "Will the book lie flat, hands-free, without force?"
2. "Is the binding sewn, and can a sewn spine open flat at this trim — or do you recommend layflat PUR?"
3. "Can you produce a blank bound dummy, then a physical proof, before the run?"
4. "What uncoated off-white stock do you carry, and how does it hold ink?"
5. "What is the turnaround for ~50 units, and what are your true binding minimums?"
