# Pricing

> Status: v0.2 — market + COGS research complete; retail price recommended, pending final decision.
> Positioning rule: On Record is a serious, permanent, numbered instrument — not a commodity notebook. Price to signal that. Underpricing actively undercuts the seriousness narrative.

## 1. Where the market sits (2025–2026, USD)

**Comparable premium guided / accountability journals & planners — $28–45 (most $28–40):**
- BestSelf *Self Journal* (fabric hardcover, lay-flat, 13-week) — **$36** (closest structural comp)
- Full Focus Planner (linen, quarterly) — **$45**
- Baron Fig *Confidant* (clothbound, lays flat) — **$28**
- Theme System Journal (clothbound, quarter-length) — **$20**
- Intelligent Change *Five Minute Journal* — **$29–37**; *Productivity Planner* — **$27**
- Clever Fox Pro — **$34–40**; Panda Planner Pro — **$30**
- Leuchtturm1917 A5 / Moleskine Classic (blank benchmark) — **$22–29**

**High-end / leather / heirloom — $60–190:**
- Hobonichi Techo Cousin / HON book — **$62–70** (+ leather cover $54–129)
- Moleskine leather — **$70–90**
- William Hannah leather disc-bound ("last notebook you'll ever need") — **$128–150**

**Numbered / capped fine editions — $355–1,500:**
- Folio Society hand-numbered limited editions — **$355–$1,500** (6x–25x their own standard edition)
- Brandon Sanderson leatherbound Kickstarter, signed + numbered top tier (capped 950) — **$500**
- These are leather literary tomes, not a direct comp — but they show what hand-numbering + a hard cap unlocks.

**Read:** the accountability-journal category has a soft ceiling near **$45**. Breaking it requires a *hard* premium signal — a numbered, capped run and genuine craft (cloth, silver foil, sewn lay-flat) — not just a nice finish. On Record has exactly those signals.

## 2. Production cost (COGS) — full spec: cloth case + silver foil + Smyth-sewn + lay-flat

Lay-flat is delivered *by* the Smyth sewing, so it is not a separate line item. Silver foil = one-time die (~$250) + per-unit impression.

| Run size | Route | Per-unit COGS |
|---|---|---|
| **~50 (seed)** | Short-run cloth casebind (Gorham-class) + foil | **~$40–48/unit** + ~$250 foil die |
| ~50 (alt) | True hand-bound buckram | $70–200/unit — *not viable at this count* |
| **~250** | Overseas offset, foil + sewing bundled | **~$6–10/unit** |
| **~500** | Overseas offset | **~$4–6/unit** |

Add ~**$8–15/unit** for packaging (paired box for the set), fulfillment, and ~3% payment fees. The single biggest cost lever is run size: 250+ units cuts per-unit cost 4–8x versus the seed run.

## 3. Recommended retail

Premium-DTC guidance: target **65–78% gross margin** at scale (value-based, not cost-plus); use **clean round numbers** (never .99) on the flagship; price limited/founding editions **at or above** standard; keep set discounts to **≤5–15%** (premium curation, not a value bundle).

| Tier | Single edition | Paired set (2 + sealed Common Record) | Positioning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market-anchored | $68 | $175 | Safest, closest to comps |
| **Recommended** | **$95** | **$245** | **Numbered founding edition** |
| Heirloom / collectible | $125 | $325 | Leans hardest into scarcity |

### Why $95 / $245
- **Seed run** (~$45 COGS): $95 ≈ **53% margin** — thin but real. The seed proves the object; it is not the margin event.
- **At scale** (~$6–10 COGS): $95 ≈ **~90% margin**. The price holds across the whole lifecycle — no awkward reprice later.
- **Set** priced as premium curation: 3 volumes at full single price = $285; **$245 is a ~14% "set" nod**, the max the bundling evidence endorses. Never discount deeper.
- $95 clears the ~$45 planner ceiling decisively (signals *not a planner*) while staying under the leather-heirloom band — where a "serious austere object" belongs.
- **Caveat:** $95 asks a buyer to accept a paper-and-cloth book at a leather-object price. It only works with the *numbered, limited, permanent* story front and center — which is the entire brand.

## 4. Open decisions
1. Confirm tier: **$95 / $245** recommended.
2. Set the seed-run cap (e.g., 50 numbered copies) — the cap is the price lever; it must be genuine.
3. Decide founding-edition mechanic: numbered founding price at par/above standard, or an early-reservation intro — not both.
4. Confirm final run size (drives COGS and therefore true margin).
