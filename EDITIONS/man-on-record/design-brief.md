# Man on Record — Design Brief

**Self-contained handoff for the Man on Record edition.** Everything a designer/bindery needs is in this one file. (Canonical shared spec lives in `/RECORD/design-brief.md`; if they ever disagree, the shared files are source of truth.)

**Edition:** Man on Record · individual · 90-day
**Version:** v0.3.3 (Concept)

---

## 1. What this is
A permanent, finite, non-refillable accountability book. Not a planner or journal. It records what the holder did; it does not remind, encourage, or forgive. Governing principle: **permanence changes behavior.** Register: administrative, severe, quiet. **Delight is poison.** No decorative interest, no variation.

Man on Record and Woman on Record are **mechanically identical** — same interior, same severity. Only the cover wordmark and colour differ.

---

## 2. The object (physical spec)
- **Extent:** 90-day edition. Front matter → Initiation (Days 1–7) → 90 Daily Pages → a Weekly Escalation Page after every 7th daily (~13) → Close-Out.
- **Opening (hard requirement):** genuine **lay-flat** — stays open, flat to the gutter, hands-free.
- **Binding:** preferred = section-sewn with a lay-flat-capable spine; acceptable fallback = layflat (PUR) if sewing can't open flat at this trim. Non-refillable. **No** spiral, disc, or ring.
- **Paper:** 100–120 GSM uncoated, off-white/bone, good ink holdout (write-test it).
- **Trim:** recommendation ~140 × 210 mm (A5-ish) or 5 × 7.75 in — hand-held, upright. Confirm.

## 3. Cover (this edition)
- **Wordmark:** "Man on Record" — the only text on the cover.
- **Colourway:** **black cloth** (~#111111).
- **Finish:** **silver foil stamp** on the wordmark only (optional registered blind emboss). No other foil, no imagery, no gloss.
- **Construction:** cloth (buckram) case or heavy uncoated/duplexed board. **No matte film lamination.**

## 4. Typography
**One typeface: IBM Plex Sans** (Inter as print-licensing fallback). No pairing, no display faces. Paragraph styles only, no local overrides. Generous inner margin so writing near the gutter is comfortable.

## 5. File construction (InDesign)
One `.indd`. Sections in order: (1) Identity Standard, (2) Non-Negotiable Standards, (3) Initiation Days 1–7, (4) Daily master ×90, (5) Weekly master, (6) Close-Out. Master pages for Daily + Weekly. **Only numbering is the Day count** (and Week count on weekly pages) — no folios. No decorative elements.

## 6. Page copy (set exactly)

### 6.1 Identity Standard *(front matter, no page number)*
> **IDENTITY STANDARD**
>
> This book is a record. It is not a planner, a journal, or a companion. It does not remind you. It does not encourage you. It does not forgive you. It records what you did.
>
> You are the only author. What is written here is permanent. It cannot be erased, refilled, or restarted. Each day is counted. Each day is signed.
>
> I, ______________________________, open this record on ____ / ____ / ______.
>
> I hold myself to the standard recorded on the following pages. I understand that this record keeps no account but the true one.
>
> Signed ______________________________   Edition No. ________

### 6.2 Non-Negotiable Standards *(no page number)*
> **NON-NEGOTIABLE STANDARDS**
>
> These are the standards you will not negotiate. Write them once, in permanent ink. They do not change without consequence, and any change is recorded — never erased.
>
> You will answer to these every day. The Daily Page asks only whether they held. If any one broke, the day is recorded as broken.
>
> 1. ______________________________________________
> 2. ______________________________________________
> 3. ______________________________________________
> 4. ______________________________________________
> 5. ______________________________________________
>
> Fewer is stronger.

### 6.3 Initiation — Days 1–7 *(fixed pages, one act per page)*
Header format `INITIATION — DAY N · WORD`; one ruled response line each.
> **DAY 1 · DECLARE** — Write, in one sentence, the person you are choosing to become. Not a goal. A person.
> **DAY 2 · STANDARD** — Restate your first Non-Negotiable Standard in your own words. Why this one, first.
> **DAY 3 · COST** — Name the cost of failing this standard — to you, and to someone else. Be specific.
> **DAY 4 · WITNESS** — Name who is watching. If no one, write that.
> **DAY 5 · BASELINE** — Record where you actually stand today. No improvement. No excuse.
> **DAY 6 · OBLIGATION** — Name the single obligation you carry into Day 1. One.
> **DAY 7 · SIGN** — Read Days 1–6. If they are true, sign. The record begins tomorrow. Signed ____ ____/____/______

## 7. Paired-set note
If sold in a paired set (with Woman on Record + The Common Record), this volume additionally gets: one added Identity Standard line — *"This record may one day be witnessed by the person keeping the record beside yours. When, and whether, is not yet decided."* — and a **sealed final signature** ("DO NOT OPEN UNTIL DAY 90") that instructs the exchange. Standalone copies omit both.

## 8. Proofing (before any run)
Blank bound dummy → pen/paper write test → silver-foil draw-down on black cloth → full physical proof.

## 9. Open decisions
Exact trim; confirm the black cloth and silver-foil against physical swatches.

## 10. What NOT to do
No added fields/ornament/ruling; no second typeface; no page numbers beyond Day/Week; no motivational softening; no gloss, matte lamination, imagery, or foil beyond the silver wordmark; no cover text other than "Man on Record."
