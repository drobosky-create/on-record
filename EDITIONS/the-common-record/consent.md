# The Common Record — Consent Model

The emotional surprise is preserved in the **ritual** (the timing and form of the reveal). It is never a hidden **obligation**. A truly hidden "your private record will be handed over" would (1) break consent and (2) corrupt the honesty of the 90 days, because people write differently when they believe something is private.

## The mechanism
When sold as a **paired set**, each individual volume's Identity Standard carries one added line, placed after the standard opening copy:

> *This record may one day be witnessed by the person keeping the record beside yours. When, and whether, is not yet decided.*

Effect:
- During the 90 days, each partner knows the record *may* be seen — so they write honestly under that awareness, not a false expectation of secrecy.
- They do **not** know the timing, the sealed mechanic, or that completion by both is the trigger. The reveal stays a genuine surprise.
- At Day 90 the exchange is still **voluntary and mutual** — consent is confirmed again in the moment, not assumed.

## Note on the shared CONTENT
The shared `CONTENT/identity-standard.md` stays clean (unwitnessed, solo default). This one added line is an **edition layer** applied only to volumes sold in a paired set — it does not alter the standalone individual editions.
