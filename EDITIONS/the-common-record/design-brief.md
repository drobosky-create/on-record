# The Common Record — Design Brief

**Self-contained handoff for The Common Record edition.** Everything a designer/bindery needs is in this one file. (Canonical shared spec lives in `/RECORD/design-brief.md`; if they ever disagree, the shared files are source of truth.)

**Edition:** The Common Record · couple's · co-authored · 45-day
**Version:** v0.3.3 (Concept)

---

## 1. What this is
A couple's shared accountability book. Two people each keep their own 90-day individual record (Man on Record / Woman on Record) alone. When **both** finish, a sealed section in each individual volume reveals the final act: they exchange and read each other's records, complete a shared **Co-Initiation**, then keep a **45-day** record together.

Same austerity, typeface, paper, trim, and binding as the individual books. The structural signature throughout: **two signatures, never one** — a day, week, or close closed with a single signature is not closed. The dual signature deliberately rhymes with a vow.

---

## 2. The object (physical spec)
- **Extent:** 45-day edition. Co-Initiation → Joint Standards → 45 Daily Pages → Weekly Escalation after every 7th daily (~6) → Close-Out.
- **Opening (hard requirement):** genuine **lay-flat**.
- **Binding:** section-sewn with a lay-flat spine preferred; layflat (PUR) fallback. Non-refillable. No spiral/disc/ring.
- **Paper:** 100–120 GSM uncoated, off-white/bone, good ink holdout (write-test it).
- **Trim:** ~140 × 210 mm (A5-ish) or 5 × 7.75 in — match the individual editions. Confirm.

## 3. Cover (this edition)
- **Wordmark:** "The Common Record" — the only text on the cover.
- **Colourway:** **dark maroon cloth** (~#2E1214) — the optical blend of Man's black and Woman's oxblood; the shared book is literally the two colours bled together, neither purely his nor hers.
- **Finish:** **silver foil stamp** on the wordmark only (optional blind emboss). No other foil, no imagery, no gloss.
- **Construction:** cloth (buckram) case or heavy board. **No matte film lamination.**

## 4. Typography
**One typeface: IBM Plex Sans** (Inter fallback). Styles only, no local overrides. Generous inner margin.

## 5. How it unlocks (product context for the designer)
Sold as a **paired set**: two individual volumes + this sealed Common Record. The reveal is a physical mechanic in the *individual* volumes — their final signature is bound-in but **sealed** ("DO NOT OPEN UNTIL DAY 90"); breaking it on Day 90 instructs the exchange. The Common Record itself ships sealed/shrink-wrapped in the box, opened only after the exchange. Consent: paired-set individual volumes carry a line noting the record "may one day be witnessed," so the 90 solo days stay honest; the exchange is voluntary and mutual at the moment. If only one partner finishes, this book never unlocks.

## 6. File construction (InDesign)
One `.indd`. Sections in order: (1) Co-Initiation — three fixed spreads, (2) Joint Non-Negotiable Standards, (3) Daily master ×45, (4) Weekly master, (5) Close-Out. Master pages for Daily + Weekly. **Two signature lines** on every acknowledgment, declaration, daily, weekly, and close page. Only numbering is the Day count. No decorative elements.

## 8. Packaging
Paired box holding two individual volumes + the sealed Common Record, in the same austere, matte, dark register. No gloss, no imagery. Premium / gift / marriage tier. Note on the box that some couples should do the exchange with a counselor present.

## 9. Proofing (before any run)
Blank bound dummy → write test → silver-foil draw-down on dark-maroon cloth → **prototype the sealed reveal** (in the individual volumes) so it opens cleanly once and cannot be resealed → full physical proof.

## 10. Open decisions
Exact trim (match individuals); confirm dark-maroon cloth against swatches; bindery to confirm the sealed section is feasible in the chosen binding.

## 11. What NOT to do
No added fields/ornament; no second typeface; no page numbers beyond Day/Week; never let a page close with one signature; no gloss/lamination/imagery/foil beyond the silver wordmark; no cover text other than "The Common Record"; never make the exchange forced rather than voluntary.
