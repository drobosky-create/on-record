# The Common Record — Edition

A couple's shared volume. Two people each keep their own 90-day record alone. When **both** have completed, a sealed section reveals the final act: they exchange and read each other's records, then begin a collective record kept together.

The individual books are private discipline. The Common Record is the reckoning that follows.

## How it unlocks
- Sold as a **paired set**: two individual editions (e.g., Man on Record + Woman on Record) plus one sealed Common Record volume.
- Each partner keeps their own 90-day run independently.
- The final leaves of each individual volume are a **sealed signature** marked *"Do not open until Day 90"* — see [`reveal-spec.md`](reveal-spec.md).
- On Day 90, breaking the seal instructs the exchange. The Common Record then begins.

## Failure paths (design for them)
- **Only one finishes:** The Common Record never unlocks. The completed partner's record stays private. No punishment, no pressure.
- **The exchange is voluntary and mutual** at the moment of reveal — an invitation, not a command. Packaging notes that some couples should do this with a counselor present.

## Consent model (non-negotiable)
The surprise is the *ritual*, never the *obligation*. See [`consent.md`](consent.md): the paired set's individual volumes carry one added line acknowledging the record may one day be witnessed — so the 90 days stay honest and the eventual sharing is a chosen act, not a trap. Only the *timing and form* of the reveal are a surprise.

- **Colourway:** **dark maroon cloth** (~#2E1214) — the optical blend of Man's black and Woman's oxblood. The shared book is literally the two colours bled together: neither purely his nor hers. Reads near-black with an oxblood cast in the light.

## Packaging
Paired box holding two individual volumes + the sealed Common Record, in the same austere, matte, dark register as the individual packaging. No gloss, no imagery. This is the premium / gift / marriage tier.
