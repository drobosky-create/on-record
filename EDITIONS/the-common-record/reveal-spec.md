# The Common Record — Sealed Reveal (production spec)

The reveal is a physical mechanic, not an instruction the reader could stumble on early. It must feel deliberate, austere, and permanent.

## The seal (in each individual volume of a paired set)
- The **final signature** (last folded section) of each individual volume is **bound in but sealed shut** — a glued fore-edge / French-fold section, or a wrapped-and-wax-sealed leaf — so it cannot be read without deliberately breaking it.
- Sealed section is marked on the outside only: **"DO NOT OPEN UNTIL DAY 90."** No other text visible.
- Breaking the seal is irreversible by design (mirrors permanence).

## Behind the seal
> You have kept ninety days.
> There is one thing left.
> If the person keeping the record beside yours has also reached this page, exchange your records now. Read. Then begin The Common Record together.
> If they have not finished, this stays closed to them. Wait, or keep your record your own.

## Production notes
- Bindery must confirm a sealed section is feasible in the chosen lay-flat/sewn construction without compromising the rest of the block.
- Prototype the seal on the **blank bound dummy** (see [`../../PRINT/print-spec.md`](../../PRINT/print-spec.md)); confirm it opens cleanly once and cannot be resealed.
- The Common Record volume itself ships sealed/shrink-wrapped separately in the paired box, opened only after the exchange.
